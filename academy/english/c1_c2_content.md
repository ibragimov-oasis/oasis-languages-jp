# English C1-C2 Academy Content

## C1 Advanced Level

### Grammar Topics

#### 1. Advanced Tenses
**Past Perfect Continuous**
- Structure: had + been + verb-ing
- Example: *She had been waiting for two hours before he arrived.*
- Usage: Emphasizes duration of an action before another past event

**Future Perfect Continuous**
- Structure: will + have + been + verb-ing
- Example: *By next month, I will have been working here for five years.*
- Usage: Emphasizes duration of an action up to a future point

**Mixed Conditionals**
- Combining different time references
- Example: *If I had studied harder (past), I would be successful now (present).*

#### 2. Advanced Modal Verbs
**Modals for Deduction**
- must have + past participle (strong deduction)
  - *He must have forgotten* (I'm almost certain he forgot)
- could/may/might have + past participle (possibility)
  - *She may have seen the email*

**Modals for Criticism**
- should have + past participle
  - *You should have called me* (but you didn't)
- shouldn't have + past participle
  - *He shouldn't have said that*

#### 3. Advanced Conditionals
**First Conditional (Real Future)**
- If + present simple, will + infinitive
- Example: *If you are late, I will be angry.*

**Second Conditional (Unreal Present)**
- If + past simple, would + infinitive
- Example: *If they had time, they would go on holiday.*

**Third Conditional (Unreal Past)**
- If + past perfect, would have + past participle
- Example: *We would have won if we had played better.*

#### 4. Inversion with Negative Adverbials
- Never have I seen...
- Rarely do we encounter...
- Not only did he arrive late, but he also...
- Under no circumstances should you...

#### 5. Ellipsis and Substitution
Making sentences more concise and sophisticated by omitting repeated elements:
- *"Would you like coffee?" "I'd love to."* (ellipsis of "have coffee")
- *"She's talented, and so am I."* (substitution with "so")

## C2 Proficiency Level

### Grammar Topics

#### 1. The Subjunctive Mood
Used in formal contexts to express wishes, suggestions, or demands:
- It's vital that a leader **be** confident (not "is")
- I suggest that he **take** the opportunity (not "takes")
- It's essential that she **arrive** on time (not "arrives")

**Common triggers:**
- It's important/vital/essential/crucial that...
- I recommend/suggest/propose that...
- It's imperative that...

#### 2. Perfect Participles and Perfect Infinitives

**Perfect Participles**
- Having + past participle
- Example: *Having finished the work, she left.*
- Shows one action completed before another

**Perfect Infinitives**
- To have + past participle
- Example: *To have completed the project on time was remarkable.*
- Refers to a completed action in the past

#### 3. Advanced Cleft Sentences

**What-Cleft (Pseudo-cleft)**
- Emphasizes information
- Example: *What I need is a good night's sleep.*
- Example: *What annoys me is people being late.*

**It-Cleft**
- Emphasizes specific information
- Example: *It was John who called* (not someone else)
- Example: *It's money that causes problems* (not other things)

#### 4. Discourse Markers

**Adding Information:**
- Furthermore, Moreover, In addition to this
- What's more, Not to mention

**Contrasting:**
- Nevertheless, Nonetheless, That said
- Be that as it may, Even so

**Result:**
- Consequently, As a result, Accordingly
- Hence, Thus, Therefore

**Sequencing:**
- In the first place, To begin with
- Subsequently, Ultimately

### Skills Development

#### Critical Reading
- Analyzing complex arguments
- Identifying bias and perspective
- Evaluating evidence quality
- Synthesizing information from multiple sources

#### Advanced Speaking
- Presenting controversial opinions diplomatically
- Using hedging language (It seems, It could be argued, Arguably)
- Employing rhetorical devices
- Achieving near-native fluency

#### Professional Communication
- Formal presentations
- Business negotiations
- Academic discussions
- Diplomatic language

## Assessment Criteria

### C1 Level Indicators
- Can understand a wide range of demanding, longer texts
- Can express ideas fluently and spontaneously
- Can use language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes
- Can produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects

### C2 Level Indicators
- Can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read
- Can summarize information from different sources
- Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently and precisely
- Can differentiate finer shades of meaning even in complex situations
